Introduction

Multilabel learning is a supervised problem that requires the prediction of more than one label category for each input instance. Unlike single label classification (binary or multiclass) where each instance is associated with only one label, multilabel classification require one to assign more than one label to each instance [Jesse2014][Yeh2017]. Multilabel learning is applicable to a wide variety of domains, such as text and audio categorization[LiLi2015], images captioning and tagging[], clinical diagnosis[], energy disaggregation [] and bionformatics []

Reference

  • Yeh2017: Chih-Kuan Yeh, Wei-Chieh Wu, Wei-Jen Ko, Yu-Chiang Frank Wang, Learning Deep Latent Spaces for Multi-Label Classification.
  • Jesse2014: Jesse Read, Fernando Perez-Cruz, Deep Learning for Multi-label Classification.
  • LiLi2015: Li Li Baobao Chang, Shi Zhao, Lei Sha, Xu Sun and Houfeng Wang, Multi-label Text Categorization with Joint Learning Predictions-as-Features Method, Proceedings of the 2015 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing
